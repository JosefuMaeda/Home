SDG 1: No Poverty

Aims to eradicate extreme poverty for all people, and reduce the proportion of people living in poverty according to national definitions by 2030. It emphasizes ensuring equal access to economic resources, basic services, and opportunities for everyone, particularly for the poorest and most vulnerable. This goal also seeks to strengthen social protection systems, enhance resilience to disasters, and provide equitable access to property ownership, land control, natural resources, and new technologies. Addressing systemic inequalities and promoting inclusive economic growth are central to achieving a world free of poverty.

SDG 2: Zero Hunger

Focuses on eliminating hunger and ensuring that everyone, everywhere, has access to sufficient and nutritious food. By 2030, it aims to achieve food security and end all forms of malnutrition, particularly for children and vulnerable populations. This goal also emphasizes promoting sustainable agriculture practices that increase productivity, preserve ecosystems, and strengthen the capacity of small-scale farmers. Efforts include improving access to technology, land, and resources, while addressing the impacts of climate change on food systems and ensuring resilience to future challenges.

SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being

Seeks to ensure universal access to healthcare, improve health systems, and promote mental and physical well-being globally. It aims to reduce maternal mortality, end preventable deaths of newborns and children under five, and combat diseases such as AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria. Additionally, it emphasizes addressing mental health issues, reducing substance abuse, and providing universal access to sexual and reproductive healthcare. Strengthening the capacity of countries to prevent, detect, and manage health risks is also a critical component of this goal.
